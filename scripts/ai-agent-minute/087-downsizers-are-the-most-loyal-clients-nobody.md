Here's what most agents miss. A senior leaving a home they've lived in for thirty years isn't a routine transaction. It's one of the most emotionally complex experiences of their life — tied up in estate planning, family dynamics, logistics, sometimes medical considerations, and the grief of leaving a place full of decades of memory. Most agents treat it like any other listing. They get through the paperwork. They miss everything else. And the client tells their children about the agent who made a hard moment harder. It should include: the emotional stages of leaving a long-term home, the financial considerations unique to downsizing, resources for estate sale services and senior relocation specialists, senior-friendly housing options in your area, and a full transition checklist from initial conversation to move-out day. Give this guide to every relevant client. You become the agent who actually prepared.
