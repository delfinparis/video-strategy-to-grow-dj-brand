Here's an uncomfortable truth about real estate: Most agents don't fail because they lack skills. They fail because they can't psychologically handle rejection.

When you conflate a "no" to a business call with a "no" to you as a person, every prospecting dial becomes a potential emotional wound. So agents stop dialing. They check email instead. They reorganize their desks. They do anything except the one activity that actually grows their business.

The reality? Most rejections have nothing to do with your competence. The prospect might be dealing with a family crisis, already working with someone, or simply not ready to move. But in the moment, it feels personal.

Here's a practical solution I've started recommending: After a tough prospecting session, use Claude AI to reframe the interaction.
