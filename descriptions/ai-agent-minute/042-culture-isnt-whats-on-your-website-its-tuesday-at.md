### LINKEDIN

Most real estate teams believe they have great culture. They genuinely mean it when they talk about it in recruiting conversations. But here's the uncomfortable truth: if you haven't defined your culture in writing, it doesn't actually exist.

Culture by default becomes whoever's having the worst day. An agent who's struggling sets the emotional tone. Conflicts go unresolved because there's no process. New team members don't know what's expected, and veterans don't know what gets celebrated.

D.J. Paris from Keeping It Real Podcast points out what this really costs us: top agents choose teams based on culture as much as commission structures. They're comparing notes with each other. Your reputation travels faster than your marketing.

The solution isn't another mission statement. It's a working culture document that defines celebrated behaviors, unacceptable actions, conflict resolution processes, and what success actually looks like on your team. When culture is written down, it becomes a recruiting tool, retention strategy, and accountability system all in one.
